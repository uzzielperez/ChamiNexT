## Slide 2 — The problem (engineers)

### Interview prep is broken for the post-AI job market

| Pain | Reality today |
|------|----------------|
| **Wrong skill** | LeetCode optimizes recall; modern roles require reasoning, communication, and shipping under AI-assisted workflows |
| **No portable proof** | A green check on HackerRank does not prove you can deploy a feature in 24–72 hours |
| **Expensive mocks** | Human mock interviews cost €50–150 each; ChatGPT alone has no rubric, no employer-facing profile |
| **Fragmented loop** | Practice, portfolio, and employer signal live in different tools |

**Who feels this most:** Junior–mid software engineers, career switchers, and AI/ML engineers targeting **€80k–€150k+** roles (US: $100k–$500k).

## Slide 4 — Our thesis

### Previous (v1)

> **Shipping beats solving. Reasoning beats memorization. Real output is the only signal that matters.**

### Evolved (v1.1) — adds the *why*, keeps the *how*

> **Misallocated talent is a civilization problem. Puzzle theater is how we misallocate it.**  
> **Shipping beats solving. Reasoning beats memorization. Real output is the only signal that matters.**

The evaluation engine is the same. The evolved thesis names the connector: brilliant people filtered by the wrong tests into the wrong rooms — including rooms where humanity's hardest problems (drug discovery, climate, conservation, frontier science) can't find them.

ChamiNext is **not** a LeetCode clone. We are a **daily practice + evaluation platform** where:

1. Engineers run **adaptive AI interviews** (Socratic, rubric-scored).
2. Engineers complete **Ship Tests**—24h / 72h / 7-day builds with PM briefs and AI review.
3. Employers use **Interview Studio** to assign challenges, score thinking + shipping, and rank shortlists.

**One platform:** Practice → talent profile → hire.

### What we measure (the strong signal)

> **Can this person go from an ambiguous technical claim → a validated, defensible conclusion → something shippable under pressure — while an interviewer adversarially probes their assumptions — with AI as co-pilot, not crutch?**

This is **not** a persona. It is the portable artifact we produce that LeetCode green checks and HackerRank scores cannot.

| Rubric dimension | What it catches | Why incumbents miss it |

| **Decomposition** | Clarifies constraints before coding | Throughput ≠ thinking |
| **Communication** | Explains trade-offs clearly | Binary pass/fail |
| **Ship proof** | Deployed URL in 24–72h | No standardized credential |

## Slide 9b — Ecosystem positioning (partners & incumbents)

### One line that keeps us in the stack

**ChamiNext is the readiness and validation layer on top of the prep and screening stack — not a replacement for it.**

We are **downstream signal** (reasoning, AI fluency, ship proof), not **upstream volume** (pattern banks, async screens). Incumbents rarely kill startups for being better; they react when they feel **budget theft** or **brand replacement**. We avoid both.

### The hiring stack (layer cake)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  VOLUME LAYER — they own this                               │
│  LeetCode / NeetCode · HackerRank / CodeSignal / CoderPad   │
└──────────────────────────────┬──────────────────────────────┘
                               │ patterns & screen pass
                               ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐

        MATCHING LAYER (peers, not enemies)
        Dex / talent agents · Mercor / expert marketplaces
```
